A design and feasibility study of Building Integrated Photo-Voltaic system on EEMCS complex
Dec 2013 - Aug 2014

In this project, a methodology is developed to determine the feasibility of Building Integrated Photo-Voltaics (BIPV) on any building and the same is applied on EEMCS complex of TU Delft. The methodology contains the following elements: (i) analysis of eligible surfaces on the building; (ii) performing shading analysis on each surface with the help of SketchUp 3D program; (iii) working out annual solar irradiation on these surfaces; (iv) choosing optimal PV modules and modelling module temperature and its electrical characteristics; (v) and optimizing the PV system for maximum electricity yield by grouping the modules to an appropriate inverter. All the above simulations are performed with the help of Python.

Technical University, Delft
Design of an autonomous off grid system involving distributed renewable energy sources
Feb 2013 - Apr 2013

- The end goal of this project is to design an autonomous off grid power system capable of providing energy to 10 households, using renewable energies such as Wind, Solar and Bio fuels.
- The components such as Wind turbine generator, Bio fuels generator and Battery has been modeled with the help of Matlab/Simulink. A control system was also designed to control various power flows.
- The model has been run for various scenarios such as normal and extreme operational conditions and were used to prove the reliability of the model.
